# Blog Storyline — 53% of Your Software Licenses Sit Unused. Your AI Budget Is Making the Same Bet Blind.

**Author:** Ai For Humans  ·  **Angle:** The practical play

**Hook:** Real talk for a second. Right now, roughly 53% of the software licenses your company pays for are barely touched or completely unused — bleeding up to $80.6M a year at large enterprises, with IT controlling just 15% of that SaaS spend. Now watch that exact same buying reflex get pointed at AI, where 59% of companies already spend over $1M a year on it.
**Villain:** The shelfware reflex: buying AI tools the way companies bought SaaS licenses — acquiring capability and hoping usage follows. It's why, of $30-40B in enterprise GenAI investment, about 95% of pilots produced zero measurable P&L impact, and 56% of 4,454 CEOs told PwC AI delivered neither cost nor revenue benefit.
**Shift:** The 5% that create real value didn't buy better models. They wired AI into one specific, high-value process instead of bolting more tools onto the org chart. And the spend that actually pays back isn't in the flashy sales-and-marketing pilots — it's in the unglamorous back-office work: document review, reconciliation, customer support, where MIT tracked $2-10M in annual savings and 30% cuts in outside agency spend.
**Hero:** This is where AI ValueFinder comes in — a 3-day engagement that starts on your operations floor, not in a boardroom, mapping where your people actually lose hours copying and pasting between systems. It hands leadership a board-ready roadmap of 3-5 projects ranked by real ROI, so you're funding workflows with a measurable payback instead of licenses nobody opens.
**Proof:** One large biotech manufacturer came in with 30+ scattered AI ideas and several failed pilots — the classic shelfware pattern, just with 'AI' on the invoice. In three days, ValueFinder filtered that pile to 5 projects tied to their own operational reality: an 11-day compliance handoff made autonomous, a 4-person manual reconciliation replaced. Three went live and surfaced $70M+ in identified ROI, $50M+ in annual savings. One engagement of many.
**Learning:** You don't have an AI budget problem. You have a shelfware habit wearing a new invoice.
**CTA:** If your team has bought plenty of AI and still can't point to the line on the P&L where it paid off, spend three days finding out which three workflows actually would — before your IT and compliance teams get handed another tool to vet.
