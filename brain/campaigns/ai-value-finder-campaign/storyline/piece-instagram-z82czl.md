# Instagram Storyline — 40+ rare diseases diagnosed. One hospital. One focused AI workflow.

**Author:** Doctor  ·  **Angle:** The outcome/case

**Hook:** A child with symptoms no textbook matches. A specialist consult two weeks out. A case file that grows while the diagnosis does not. This is what a rare disease looks like inside a hospital — not a data problem, a clock running against a patient. The cost of that gap is measured in beds held and answers not given.
**Villain:** AI spend that spreads across everything and lands on nothing. Of $30–40B in enterprise GenAI investment, roughly 95% of pilots produced no measurable P&L impact. Budgets chase visible functions and generic tool rollouts while the workflow that actually holds the stakes waits its turn.
**Shift:** The 5% that captured value did not buy better models. They wired AI tightly into one real process. The winners use AI to redesign a workflow, not to bolt more tools onto the ones they already have.
**Hero:** Techolution's AI ValueFinder — a 3-day engagement that starts on the ops floor, not the boardroom, and comes back with 3-5 projects ranked by enterprise ROI. It does what a triage nurse does: sorts the caseload by which one, if fixed, changes the outcome. It picks the workflow before anyone commits to production-scale spend.
**Proof:** Boston Children's Hospital embedded AI in a single defined workflow and diagnosed over 40 rare disease cases, cutting operational burden and moving faster to answers. One focused process, deployed. It is the same pattern behind a biotech manufacturer that filtered 30+ scattered AI ideas down to 5 board-ready projects, deployed 3, and identified $70M+ in ROI — one of many.
**Learning:** AI does not save a patient by existing everywhere. It saves one by working in the right place.
**CTA:** For leadership teams sitting on a list of AI ideas and no map of which one pays — three days is enough to find out where the value actually lives.
