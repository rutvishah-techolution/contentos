# LinkedIn Storyline — 30 AI Ideas, Several Failed Pilots, 3 Days: How One Biotech Found the 5 That Mattered

**Author:** Interviewer  ·  **Angle:** The outcome/case (Type 1): how a large biotech manufacturer with 30+ scattered AI ideas and several failed pilots got filtered to 5 board-ready projects in 3 days, deployed 3, and turned an 11-day compliance handoff autonomous — $50M+ annual savings. Villain: pilot sprawl with no ranked map. Hero enters after the pain: AI ValueFinder + FEA shown through the client's operational change.

**Hook:** A large biotech manufacturer had committed to AI early. It had over 30 ideas in motion, a leadership team convinced of the technology, and several completed pilots. It also had nothing on the P&L to show for any of it. This is not a story about weak technology. Across $30–40 billion in enterprise GenAI investment, roughly 95% of pilots delivered no measurable P&L impact. The biotech was inside that 95%, and its leadership knew it.
**Villain:** Pilot sprawl with no ranked map. Thirty-plus initiatives running in parallel, each defensible in isolation, none tied to a specific high-value workflow — spend distributed across whatever function was most visible rather than where operational reality made return recoverable. MIT's data shows this exact pattern: budget aimed at visible functions while higher-return back-office processes go untouched.
**Shift:** The winners in that 5% are not distinguished by better models. They are distinguished by tight integration into a real business process. The failure was never a technology-selection problem. It was a mapping problem — the company was choosing use cases from a shortlist of what looked promising, not from an audit of where its own operations quietly bled value.
**Hero:** AI ValueFinder — a 3-day engagement that starts on the operations floor rather than the boardroom — put the biotech's actual workflows, not its idea backlog, under examination. It filtered the 30-plus initiatives down to 5 projects ranked by enterprise ROI, board-ready at the end of three days. Full Enterprise Automation then took three of those five operational across department lines, including an 11-day compliance handoff — the manual approval chain that moved a batch record between quality, regulatory, and production — rebuilt to run autonomously.
**Proof:** Three deployed projects. One 11-day compliance handoff made autonomous. A four-person manual reconciliation replaced. $70M+ in identified ROI and $50M+ in annual savings — from a company that three days earlier had 30 ideas and no ranked way to choose among them. One of several engagements following the same sequence: audit the operational reality first, deploy against the ranked map second.
**Learning:** The 5% that capture value did not pick better tools. They mapped spend to their own operational reality instead of the shortlist leadership started with.
**CTA:** For leadership teams sitting on more AI initiatives than they can rank — the first move is a map built from your operations, not your idea list.
