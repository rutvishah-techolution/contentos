# Blog — Final

# 53% of Your Software Licenses Sit Unused. Your AI Budget Is About to Make the Same Bet Blind.

Roughly **53% of the software licenses your company pays for are barely touched or completely unused**, bleeding up to $80.6M a year at large enterprises. IT controls just 15% of that SaaS spend. Now watch that same buying reflex get pointed at AI, where 59% of companies already spend over $1M a year.

You see where this is going.

## The habit you didn't know you had

The villain isn't a vendor. It's a reflex.

Companies bought SaaS the way you'd stock a supply closet: acquire the capability, hope the usage shows up later. It rarely did. Now that same instinct has a new invoice with "AI" on it.

The numbers are brutal. Of $30–40B in enterprise GenAI investment, about **95% of pilots produced zero measurable P&L impact**, according to MIT. PwC asked 4,454 CEOs how AI performed. 56% said it delivered *neither* cost nor revenue benefit.

That's not an AI problem. That's shelfware wearing a costume.

## What the 5% did differently

The companies that made money didn't buy better models. They wired AI into one specific, high-value process instead of bolting more tools onto the org chart.

The payback wasn't in the flashy sales-and-marketing pilots that get funded first. It was in the unglamorous back-office grind. Document review. Reconciliation. Customer support. MIT tracked **$2–10M in annual savings** and **30% cuts in outside agency spend** in exactly those quiet corners.

The money was hiding in the work nobody wanted to demo.

## Start on the floor, not in the boardroom

**AI ValueFinder** is a short engagement that starts on your operations floor, watching where your people actually lose hours copying and pasting between systems. It ends with a board-ready roadmap: **3–5 projects ranked by real ROI**.

One large biotech manufacturer walked in with 30+ scattered AI ideas and several stalled pilots. The shelfware pattern, just with "AI" on the invoice. Days later, that pile was filtered to 5 projects tied to *their* operational reality: an 11-day compliance handoff made autonomous, a 4-person manual reconciliation replaced. Three went live. **$70M+ in identified ROI, $50M+ in annual savings.**

One honest caveat. Whatever workflow you pick, run it through your IT security and compliance teams before you connect real enterprise data. Speed is great. A data mess is not.

## The line to remember

You don't have an AI budget problem. You have a shelfware habit wearing a new invoice.

For leadership teams ready to find which three workflows would actually pay off, three days on the floor is where that answer lives.
