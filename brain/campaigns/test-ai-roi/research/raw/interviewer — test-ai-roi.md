# Interviewer — Raw Research

## Summary

The AI landscape in 2026 is characterized by a critical shift from experimental pilots to a demand for demonstrable Return on Investment (ROI), particularly with the rise of agentic AI systems. While AI adoption is near-universal, a significant gap exists between deployment and measurable financial impact, pushing enterprises to focus on specific business problems and robust governance. The rapid pace of AI innovation is creating a two-track labor market, emphasizing unique human skills, and simultaneously raising profound ethical and societal questions that demand urgent regulatory and collaborative solutions.
