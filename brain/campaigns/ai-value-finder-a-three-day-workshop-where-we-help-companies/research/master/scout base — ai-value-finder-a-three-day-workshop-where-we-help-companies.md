# Scout Research Base
## Overview

AI adoption across large enterprises is now near-universal, yet measurable returns remain the exception. The findings below trace a consistent gap between AI ambition and AI outcomes: high board-level attention and near-total deployment, but weak ROI, stalled pilots, missing strategy, and structural barriers in systems and workforce readiness. Where returns do appear, they correlate with clarity of use case, governance, and process-level workflow mapping — the core problem space a workflow-mapping-to-roadmap engagement addresses.

## Enterprise AI Adoption Has Reached Board-Level Saturation

AI is now embedded in the strategic narrative of America's largest companies. 85% of Fortune 500 companies mention AI in their annual filings, up from 29% in 2022, and total AI mentions across all filings grew 601% ([source](https://dev.to/riospablo/fortune-500-ai-disclosure-analysis-how-americas-largest-companies-talk-about-ai-in-sec-filings-5531)).

Deployment tracks disclosure: 91% of businesses use AI in at least one capacity in 2026 ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQHc0nJPHljY_wADDsEQmMdIYKJPAW815_zim1K8IrhprKFPjT9xblJq3QLH2u7C_50NdtCyQAhRTGeJKnyXOgr5bTrWX8R-fFfkY4Djs8n7KJJBzoWyVOGcGAJsP9MkpubYVbP5OSYxp4rtsFL2-Vg==)). At the C-suite, urgency is high: 99% of C-suite executives have rebuilt or significantly changed their AI strategy within the last 24 months, with 67% believing their current business model may not be viable by 2029 due to AI disruption ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQHPse4DFxXngNLZKN9fHJXeaaXdWUHMeT6XGw4HlO2UuonRSmysxRMaSEOIuNNo7xu4kC61Hf5mp_jIRnn11dSTfrlGFyNvIyST-TtxKG1fbumCGxICPQWOJdUZSbLQt2du97-mbquJn4QC-vYk_t53lsLDMrSyUwbY96TKslcW8dz2LGJIz1HNpzkrR_E0hWispzFoh2t8Fwi32VF76p1i4QpxtnJpqpjmE=)).

## The ROI Gap: Investment Without Return

Despite saturation, most AI spending is not producing measurable results. 42% of corporate AI initiatives yielded zero ROI in 2025, up from 17% the year before, and 88% of AI pilots never make it to production — meaning only about 1 in 8 prototypes becomes an operational capability ([source](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/why-42-percent-of-ai-projects-show-zero-roi-and-how-to-be-in-the-58-percent)). A separate figure holds that 87% of AI projects never reach production, and up to 70% of companies see minimal impact from AI ([source](https://www.techclass.com/resources/learning-and-development-articles/risks-of-poor-ai-adoption-in-businesses-common-mistakes-to-avoid)).

The returns that do exist are narrow. Only 25.5% of AI pilots or deployments achieved measurable P&L impact ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGM298VyvZOQEuCfUClGt7hdtvHUS15xSDMNFeiD7DRs2apG74hNJfBH6HRONa4WQCv2Wm7Y4Y5rwRFzD9ifSb8nXcSjJOs6Hby_cIJAgosvMaEf13adwhjn2xFFM-oMOrnwVHX-gC-Bf5eLWOQ==)), and McKinsey finds only 39% of organizations report any EBIT impact from AI ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQHc0nJPHljY_wADDsEQmMdIYKJPAW815_zim1K8IrhprKFPjT9xblJq3QLH2u7C_50NdtCyQAhRTGeJKnyXOgr5bTrWX8R-fFfkY4Djs8n7KJJBzoWyVOGcGAJsP9MkpubYVbP5OSYxp4rtsFL2-Vg==)). MIT reported that approximately 95% of organizations investing in Generative AI are seeing little or no measurable return ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQG4fWKqeAd4Xgy-IBbcPtVuxJ3jsRk7l0vVIT0fieY8TtU2MhTxcPIeJmUu2duWvURQnHtjNImlLvkjhoJNGBXLCXku6a7c5_8odvJOxBR4rl_9pKyytoYApYlTMVVSabN4cHMo6gXsvX87PlV2lW5wuv1vgb6R8jbQ0YRMxJBWyn_9bLKeyV8qAfmb9sQyK7IWg0mG_-Mktd1iQyCUq2GpZ-ulkCWo4yMq1w==)).

Companies also struggle to even measure outcomes. Only 51% quantify the results of their AI adoption ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQEK6lBrwsKVCdEmLA7pi64Du6zyNhXQaON4vNJc5kxJfrEC7qzrWakLaOetORXR_0o0kZZXToS0PhqNT5VFOjj_RW1-mXX6VVkYu0g-3GKVrXmePkhK1i890V4KWt93hSJcqMWjqxcmCgqxr25s2TjDNkhsRvht3zOqxwI==)), and only 7% of business leaders report having established a clear ROI from their AI investments ([source](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4DsyB_Jve_g)). One report frames the waste directly: 72% of enterprise AI investments are currently destroying value ([source](https://larridin.com/blog/ai-roi-measurement)).

## Root Cause: No Strategy, No Use-Case Clarity

The dominant failure mode is not technology but the absence of a defined plan. 85% of CEOs anticipate major business transformation from AI, but only 1 in 5 have a plan ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQFsjq5jZwEsP-8gAskToQg5Y0UP54OaUfIt35tiYb8N2x9FlUZ3CRTl4i9P_6Ol3mah4oMXClnmyPKJbtXRZr4Yt0PhJLfAFgHx1ivDtcwUNFPQOdkygZXsvGTkt0UIZlOVLG4Wrl0prn7MAEwWR9zfUdW8eBiIIxD2lQiE6yUxJlLztsFpj7I1boo2s7L1MRIz0XHcQ==)). Lack of clear strategy and objectives is the biggest reason AI initiatives fail, and Gartner projects over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 due to escalating costs and unclear business value ([source](https://www.techclass.com/resources/learning-and-development-articles/risks-of-poor-ai-adoption-in-businesses-common-mistakes-to-avoid)).

The same clarity gap blocks adoption on the ground: the leading barrier to AI adoption among employees is a lack of clarity around its use case or value proposition ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQFsjq5jZwEsP-8gAskToQg5Y0UP54OaUfIt35tiYb8N2x9FlUZ3CRTl4i9P_6Ol3mah4oMXClnmyPKJbtXRZr4Yt0PhJLfAFgHx1ivDtcwUNFPQOdkygZXsvGTkt0UIZlOVLG4Wrl0prn7MAEwWR9zfUdW8eBiIIxD2lQiE6yUxJlLztsFpj7I1boo2s7L1MRIz0XHcQ==)).

## Structural and Workforce Barriers

Beyond strategy, execution stalls on infrastructure and people. Fragmented systems, disconnected workflows, and integration debt are the real barriers to AI ROI: 59% of organizations struggle with integration complexity from point-to-point connections, and 65% struggle with integrating legacy systems and modern cloud platforms ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGM298VyvZOQEuCfUClGt7hdtvHUS15xSDMNFeiD7DRs2apG74hNJfBH6HRONa4WQCv2Wm7Y4Y5rwRFzD9ifSb8nXcSjJOs6Hby_cIJAgosvMaEf13adwhjn2xFFM-oMOrnwVHX-gC-Bf5eLWOQ==)).

A workforce enablement gap compounds this. While 82% of C-suite leaders claim their organizations use AI solutions in workflows, only 34% have equipped employees with AI tools; and although about 80% of C-suite executives say they provide AI training several times a year, most professionals report receiving no training at all ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQF3-YJfOnNTcTcX84SM2p35Z4GyARdQEvhj8-1FAAy_Xj85IJGIE-UpL154MD1IhSkFatZDlBbkZZ6S5g3DaTTU0wO2MxYf_yRaHuoB5OVdiYpHZT91RFGA_K3S_nZCb-MZ5l_dywb1PNZNoKjCynqpOtSoHPYqfAafbbZ3xo0-0lEEdgw==)).

## What Works: Process-Level Focus, Governance, and Defined ROI

Returns concentrate where AI is applied to specific processes with governance in place. AI process optimization is the application of AI — including machine learning, natural language processing, generative AI, computer vision, and intelligent decision engines — to analyze, automate, and continuously improve how business processes run, including tasks once considered too complex for rule-based automation ([source](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQGahKHxgH0oO3TJMSVbhQshkCV-i6Dd-OvJofn3-XtUF6-OJf3x7mZasz2V_a2J7e_hsODHqtTT2sCBOI0cjQjLOuuiL6DsHz9P29aAdgIj5idkrgVEC7GadNM8RcAob3BJ4kTKlGCNB_LGeDk7ZWV3w==)).
