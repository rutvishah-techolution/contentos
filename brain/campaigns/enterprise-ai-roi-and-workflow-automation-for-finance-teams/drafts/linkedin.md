# LinkedIn — Final Copy

Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by the end of 2027, according to Gartner, which points to rising costs, unclear value, and weak risk controls.

For a CFO, that is not a technology story. It is a capital-allocation story.

Consider the mechanism. An orchestrated agentic workflow now costs roughly $1.20 per interaction, against about $0.04 in 2023 — a 30x jump. Uber exhausted its entire 2026 AI coding budget by April. Costs escape control faster than value can be measured, and the project gets pulled the quarter someone asks what it returned.

## Only 130 of Them Were Real

Of thousands of self-described "agentic" vendors, Gartner deems only about 130 real. The rest are rebranded chatbots. Add an estimated $800B in circular financing — where a supplier funds a lab that spends the money buying that same supplier's products — and by Q1 2026 investors are already demanding "clean" revenue. Board defensibility now rests on vendor scrutiny and governance, not on being early.

The correction is procedural, not heroic. Instead of buying an "agentic" platform on the strength of a demo, a finance team starts on its own operational floor, ranks where AI actually touches the P&L, and deploys inside one named workflow — with a contractual line most vendors avoid.

## The Number Before the Spend

Techolution's model diverges here. Its automation engine, BPA 4.0 — software that learns how expert staff actually work rather than following rigid rules — carries a guarantee: 2x quantifiable savings on a $100K engagement within 100 days, or full refund. The value is defined before the spend, not defended after it.

A large biotech manufacturer arrived with 30+ scattered AI ideas and several failed pilots. The exact profile Gartner's forecast describes. A 3-day filtering exercise cut them to 5 board-ready projects. Three shipped. One turned an 11-day compliance handoff autonomous. Another replaced a 4-person manual reconciliation. The result: $70M+ in identified ROI and $50M+ in annual savings, each figure traceable to a named workflow, not an aggregate budget line.

The projects that survive 2027 aren't the best funded. They're the ones that named the return before the spend.

For finance leaders who would rather guarantee the workflow than defend the budget: which single workflow could you put a number — and a refund clause — against?
