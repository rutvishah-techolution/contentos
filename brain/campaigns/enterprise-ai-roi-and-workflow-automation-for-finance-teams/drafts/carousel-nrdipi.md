# LinkedIn Carousel — Draft 1

### Slide 1
Real talk for a second.

Your finance team automated a dozen tasks this year.

Invoice tagging. Report formatting. Meeting prep.

And month-end close *still* takes just as long.

Here's why that gap costs more than you think →

### Slide 2
The trap: counting minutes.

95% of enterprise generative-AI pilots deliver zero measurable P&L result.

Despite $30–40B invested.
(Source: MIT report via Yahoo Finance)

And it's not the models' fault →

### Slide 3
The failures come down to integration and workflow — not the technology.

Automating a task shaves minutes off one person's day.

But the 11-day compliance handoff? The 4-person reconciliation? The multi-department close?

Those live BETWEEN the tasks.

Task automation never touches them →

### Slide 4
Here's what nobody in finance is saying out loud.

Only 17% of finance teams actually use AI in core workflows.

45% are still stuck in limited pilots.
(Source: State of AI in Finance 2026)

The winners didn't buy the most tools →

### Slide 5
They stopped automating tasks and started automating the whole workflow — end to end, across departments.

That's the difference between saving minutes and saving weeks.

One team we worked with mapped an 11-day compliance handoff.

It bounced between four departments →

### Slide 6
They made the entire chain run autonomously.

A 4-person manual reconciliation — gone.

That's Full Enterprise Automation: instead of speeding up single tasks, it takes the connected workflow operational across every department it touches.

(Honest note: before wiring this into sensitive financial data, run it past your IT security and compliance teams first.) →

### Slide 7
The results that survive an audit are the workflow-level ones.

In one engagement — a large biotech manufacturer sitting on 30+ scattered AI ideas and several failed pilots —

narrowing to a handful of connected workflows surfaced $70M+ in identifiable ROI and $50M+ in annual savings.

Not from more tools. From fixing what happened between the tools. →

### Slide 8
Automating tasks makes your people faster.

Automating workflows makes your P&L different.

→

### Slide 9
If your team has automated plenty of tasks and the close still takes the same two weeks —

that's the workflow talking, not the tools.

Worth a conversation before your next planning cycle.

**Caption:** Your finance team automated a dozen tasks this year — and month-end close still takes just as long. That gap isn't a tool problem. It's a workflow problem. 95% of enterprise AI pilots show zero P&L impact, and the failures trace back to what happens *between* the tasks: the compliance handoffs, the reconciliations, the multi-department close. Here's the difference between saving minutes and saving weeks. Swipe through. #EnterpriseAI #FinanceTransformation #WorkflowAutomation
