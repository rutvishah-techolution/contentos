# Doctor — Raw Research

## Summary

CFOs are under pressure to deploy AI, but the reality is that many initiatives are still in pilot phases with unclear ROI, especially beyond basic automation. While AI offers significant potential for cost reduction and efficiency in finance through automating repetitive tasks like invoice processing and fraud detection, the critical challenge lies in measuring actual value, ensuring data quality, and integrating AI into existing, often fragmented, systems. The real gains come from a disciplined approach that aligns AI with strategic business outcomes, rather than treating it as a magic bullet.

- **Source:** https://www.cfoconnect.eu/resources/reports/state-of-ai-in-finance-2026/

The system reduced false positives by 60% and detected two to four times more financial crimes (with greater accuracy).

- **Why it matters:** Fraud detection isn't just about saving money; it's about protecting patients—or in this case, customers—from harm. A 300% ROI with a fast payback period shows that effective AI deployments can deliver clear, measurable benefits where the stakes are high. This isn't theoretical; these are real numbers from real banks.
