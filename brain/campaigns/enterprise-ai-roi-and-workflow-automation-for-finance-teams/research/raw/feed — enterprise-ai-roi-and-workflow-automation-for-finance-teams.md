# Feed — Raw Research

## Summary

AI in finance is a mess right now. On one hand, you've got massive partnerships like OpenAI and PwC pushing agentic AI for CFOs, promising to automate everything from procurement to forecasting. On the other, companies like Microsoft are admitting their AI costs are higher than human employees, and firms are blowing through AI budgets in months. The "GenAI Paradox" is real: everyone's adopting it, but hardly anyone's seeing a measurable ROI. It's a gold rush with a massive hidden bill, and frankly, most CFOs are still trying to figure out where to even start.

## Findings

### 1. Microsoft's internal data shows that running AI agents at scale is currently more expensive than employing human workers for equivalent tasks.

- **Evidence:** Microsoft has disclosed its AI token and agent costs are exceeding human employee costs for equivalent tasks, a landmark admission from one of the largest AI investors.
- **Source:** https://fortune.com/2026/05/22/microsoft-ai-cost-problem-tokens-agents/
- **Why it matters:** This directly contradicts the core promise of AI for cost savings and headcount reduction, forcing a complete re-evaluation of current AI cost models for enterprise CFOs. If Microsoft can't make it cheaper, who can?

### 2. Enterprises are experiencing an explosion in AI inference costs due to unchecked token consumption in agentic AI workflows, making some projects economically unviable.

- **Evidence:** Royal Bank of Canada's CEO reported a 500% jump in token usage in six months, and Cisco's CEO called their usage "pretty, pretty crazy." A mysterious corporation reportedly spent $500 million in one month on Claude AI after failing to set usage limits for employees.
- **Source:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/the-token-bill-comes-due-inside-the-industry-scramble-to-manage-ais-runaway-costs/
- **Why it matters:** This "Token Anxiety" is a hidden killer for enterprise AI budgets. Companies are rushing into agentic AI without understanding the compounding costs, leading to massive, unexpected bills and projects getting cut.

### 3. ClickUp laid off 22% of its workforce (290 employees) and simultaneously deployed approximately 3,000 internal AI agents, creating a 3:1 agent-to-employee ratio.

- **Evidence:** On May 22, 2026, ClickUp cut 22% of its 1,300-person workforce and now operates 3,000 internal AI agents.
- **Source:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/25/what-clickups-mass-layoff-tells-us-about-the-future-of-work/
- **Why it matters:** This is a direct, undeniable example of "Hyper Automation" leading to headcount replacement, not just augmentation. It's a clear signal to CFOs about the potential, and the risk, of aggressive AI adoption.

### 4. OpenAI and PwC have partnered to develop AI agents for finance workflows, including planning, forecasting, procurement, and tax, with PwC building an "AI native finance function" using OpenAI as a "client zero."

- **Evidence:** OpenAI and PwC announced a collaboration on May 4, 2026, to use AI agents to automate finance functions, and PwC is building a procurement agent within OpenAI's finance organization.
- **Source:** https://openai.com/index/openai-pwc-finance-collaboration/
- **Why it matters:** This is Big Four pushing frontier AI directly into the CFO's office. It signals a major shift in how finance functions will operate, with large consulting firms acting as the primary integrators for complex AI solutions.

### 5. Despite widespread adoption, nearly 80% of companies using generative AI report no material impact on earnings, creating a "GenAI Paradox."

- **Evidence:** McKinsey found that nearly eight out of ten companies have deployed GenAI, but roughly the same percentage report no material impact on earnings. A 2025 MIT NANDA report found 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots deliver no measurable P&L impact.
- **Source:** https://techcrunch.com/video/neas-tiffany-luck-says-enterprises-are-still-figuring-out-their-ai-roi/
- **Why it matters:** Everyone's trying AI, but few are seeing real returns. This highlights a critical disconnect between experimentation and tangible business value, indicating a need for much clearer ROI frameworks beyond just token usage.

### 6. CFOs are struggling to measure AI ROI, with 36% unable to justify a return on investment and 71% citing model inaccuracy as a top concern.

- **Evidence:** A survey of 129 CFOs from December 2025 to February 2026 found that 36% cannot justify AI ROI, and 71% are concerned about model inaccuracy. Only 4% of CFOs reported a pilot success rate above 50%.
- **Source:** https://battery.com/blog/ai-adoption-cfo-suite/
- **Why it matters:** The C-suite wants AI, but they can't prove it's working. This creates a huge opportunity for clear, evidence-based ROI frameworks, but it also shows the industry is failing to deliver on its promises to finance leaders.

### 7. The shift in AI costs from per-token pricing to overall token consumption by agentic workflows is leading to higher AI bills, despite per-token prices dropping by 75% in the last year.

- **Evidence:** Per-token inference costs dropped 75% in a year, but overall AI bills are increasing due to the way agentic AI consumes tokens through multiple steps and tool calls. The number of tokens processed per quarter has grown by 17,000x over four years.
- **Source:** https://www.exponentialview.co/p/why-ai-bills-rise-as-costs-fall
- **Why it matters:** This is the classic bait-and-switch. Vendors tout falling unit costs, but the architecture of agentic AI means total spend skyrockets. CFOs need to understand this deceptive math before committing to large-scale deployments.
