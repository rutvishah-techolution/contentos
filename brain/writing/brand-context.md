# Brand Context — Techolution

The single source of truth for what the brand does, who it speaks to, how it
sounds, and what it never does. Swap this file to serve a different brand — the
storyline/write stages read the brand facts from here.

## Company
Techolution is an AI services company. It helps enterprises find where AI will
generate measurable ROI, then deploys it end-to-end across departments — not
isolated task automations, but connected workflows that cross department
boundaries and show up on the P&L. It is *not* a strategy-deck consultancy and
*not* a tool vendor. Core promise: **from scattered AI ideas to measurable
enterprise-wide impact.** Tagline: "Turn your AI into ROI."

## Products / services
- **AI ValueFinder** — a 3-day engagement with leadership; output is a board-ready
  AI roadmap built from the company's own operational reality (starts on the ops
  floor, not the boardroom). Produces 3-5 projects ranked by enterprise ROI.
- **Full Enterprise Automation (FEA)** — the deployment engine; takes the roadmap
  operational across departments. "Task automation saves minutes; workflow
  automation saves weeks."
- **BPA 4.0** — AI-first automation platform inside FEA; learns how expert
  employees actually work (not rigid if-then flows). ROI guarantee: $100K → 2x
  quantifiable savings in 100 days, or full refund.
- Always explain any product term in one plain clause. Never present engagements
  as feature lists — show them through the client's experience.

## Proof point (one of many — never the only evidence)
Large biotech manufacturer: 30+ scattered AI ideas, several failed pilots →
ValueFinder filtered to 5 board-ready projects in 3 days → 3 deployed via FEA →
**$70M+ identified ROI, $50M+ annual savings.** Lead with the operational change
(an 11-day compliance handoff made autonomous; a 4-person manual reconciliation
replaced), not the dollar figure alone.

## Target reader (ICP)
C-suite (CTO, CFO, CEO, COO) + VP-level budget owners. They scan, they're
skeptical, they have ~4 minutes, they decide in 60 seconds. They forward only
content with something they didn't already know.

**Audience funnel — one per piece:**
- **CFO:** capital allocation, risk, budget efficiency, board defensibility. Stats
  lean financial; scenes = budget/board/quarterly reviews.
- **CTO/COO:** implementation risk, vendor lock-in, execution timelines. Stats
  lean operational; scenes = ops floor, deployments, integrations.

## Voice
Confident not arrogant · specific not academic · urgent not alarmist · story-
forward not feature-forward · executive not influencer (no slang, no jokes, no
unanswered rhetorical questions). (The *persona* writing the piece sets the
detailed voice; this is the brand's overall register.)
- ✓ "Three pilots failed. The technology wasn't wrong. The map was."
- ✗ "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape, organizations face challenges."

## CTA philosophy — selector, not seller
Self-selecting, exclusive, one clear action. ✓ "For teams that have automated
plenty of tasks and are ready to automate the enterprise." ✗ "Contact us today"
/ "Schedule a demo." Vary the phrasing every time.

## Hard rules
1. Every piece connects to the brand — hero (Type 1) or credible voice (Type 2).
2. Product details live inside the story, never as a brochure.
3. Never call a company or the reader a failure — reframe to "how to get it right."
4. No orphaned jargon — one plain clause of context or cut it.
5. Proof points are plural — the biotech case is one of many.
6. Every stat is sourced and verified, or it's cut.
7. Current year is 2026 (unless citing a specific prior year).
8. ROI/stakes visible in paragraph one (first ~60 words).
9. Zero repetition — no idea appears twice.
