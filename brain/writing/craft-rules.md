# Craft Rules

A small set of voice-neutral quality rules that raise every piece regardless of
who writes it. These govern *craft*, not voice — the persona still owns tone,
vocabulary, and rhythm. Kept deliberately short to avoid competing with persona
style.

## Product/service lives inside the story
The reader learns what a service does by watching it work on a real problem —
never from a feature list. Compress any multi-step engagement (2 days, 3 days, 5
phases) to 2-3 sentences shown through what the client *experienced*.
Test: cover the product name — does the paragraph still read like a story, or a
spec sheet? If spec sheet, rewrite.

## Land the learning, then stop
When the takeaway line hits, the CTA follows immediately. No summary, no "to
recap," no restating what the reader just understood. If you feel the urge to
explain the takeaway, the line wasn't sharp enough — fix the line.

## No stat dumps
Never more than 2 statistics in a row without a narrative beat (a person, a team,
a reaction, a consequence) in between. Back-to-back stats read like a research
summary, not a story.

## Headers are hooks, not labels
Every section header should make the reader want to read what's under it. Good:
"Fifteen Automations, Zero Enterprise Impact." Bad: "Introduction," "The
Solution," "How It Works." Test: would someone click this header alone?

## Every claim ties to a business outcome
No sentence makes an AI claim with no business consequence attached in the same
paragraph. Not "AI can process unstructured data" — instead "AI processes the
invoices that currently take four people three days, ~$400K/year for one
workflow." The reader should never think "interesting, but so what?"

## No orphaned jargon
Every technical term or product name gets one plain-language clause of context, or
it doesn't appear. A CFO should never have to look a term up.

## Urgency without panic
Include a "why now," framed as opportunity cost, not fear. "Every quarter without
X costs you Y" — never "you'll be left behind."

## Zero repetition
No idea, argument, or beat appears twice — not reworded, not as a callback, not as
a concluding restatement. After drafting, delete any paragraph the reader has
already absorbed. Shorter with no repetition beats longer that restates.

## Current year is 2026
Unless referencing historical data from a specific prior year.
