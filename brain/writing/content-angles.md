# Content Angles & Quality Gates

A **storyline-stage** tool. It runs *after* research is complete and only shapes
how each content piece is framed. It does NOT touch, guide, or restrict the
research — research stays fully broad and autonomous.

Its job: turn the rich research into **distinct, sharp angles — one per piece** —
so a blog, a LinkedIn post, and an Instagram piece each say something *different*
and each is worth forwarding.

## One distinct angle per piece

The same campaign research can be told from many angles. Each piece takes a
*different* one so pieces don't repeat each other. An angle is simply the specific
lens/argument a piece leads with — usually the lens of the persona writing it.

Common angle types (a menu, not a requirement — pick whatever genuinely fits):
- **The data story** — a striking figure/finding and what it means for the reader.
- **How it actually works** — an explainer that demystifies something.
- **The trend read** — what's shifting and why it matters now.
- **The cautionary pattern** — what's quietly going wrong and how to avoid it.
- **The outcome/case** — a concrete result and the path to it.
- **The practical play** — what to actually do this week.

No forced mold. The angle should be the natural, strongest way *this persona*
would enter *this* topic.

## The three quality gates (every piece must pass all three)

These are a quality bar, not a topic filter — they restrict nothing about subject
matter.

1. **Would an executive forward this?** Not "read" — *forward, with a note.*
   Requires at least one of: an insight they didn't have, a reframing of a
   problem they thought they understood, an evidence-backed claim, or data they
   haven't seen elsewhere.
2. **Is the ROI/relevance clear within 60 seconds?** The business point — a
   figure, a consequence, a stakes-raising fact — must land in the first
   paragraph. Not "AI is important."
3. **Could a competitor publish this unchanged?** If yes, it lacks a
   brand-specific angle (a proof point, methodology, or point of view only this
   brand can make). Generic thought leadership with a logo fails.

If a piece fails a gate, sharpen the angle — don't narrow the topic.
